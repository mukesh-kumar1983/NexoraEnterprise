# ADR-002: Domain Foundation

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-05
- **Decision Makers:** NexoraEnterprise Architecture Team

---

## Context

NexoraEnterprise is being developed as an enterprise-grade, multi-tenant SaaS platform following
the principles of Domain-Driven Design (DDD) and Clean Architecture.

Before implementing business aggregates such as Tenant, Branch, Department, Employee, Payroll,
CRM, and Finance, the project requires a stable Domain Foundation that establishes consistent
patterns for entities, aggregate roots, value objects, domain events, business rules, and
auditing.

This ADR documents the architectural decisions that define the Domain Foundation and will serve
as the baseline for all future domain development.

---

# Decision

## 1. Entity Hierarchy

The Domain model adopts the following inheritance hierarchy:

```text
Entity
    ↑
AggregateRoot
    ↑
AuditableAggregateRoot
```

The previously proposed `BaseEntity` abstraction has been removed because it introduced no
behavior or responsibility beyond the `Entity` class.

`Entity` is the root abstraction for all domain entities.

---

## 2. Aggregate Roots

Only aggregate roots are responsible for:

- Maintaining aggregate consistency.
- Enforcing business invariants.
- Raising domain events.
- Exposing pending domain events through a read-only collection.

Child entities must not raise domain events directly.

Business logic outside the aggregate must interact only with the aggregate root.

---

## 3. Domain Events

Domain events represent business facts that have already occurred.

Every domain event:

- Implements `IDomainEvent`.
- Inherits from `DomainEvent`.
- Records the UTC occurrence time.
- Is raised only by an aggregate root.

Domain events remain part of the Domain layer and contain no infrastructure concerns.

---

## 4. Value Objects

Value Objects are immutable objects that represent concepts without identity.

All Value Objects:

- Derive from the `ValueObject` base class.
- Are compared using value-based equality.
- Do not contain identifiers.
- Must remain immutable after creation.

Examples include Email Address, Money, Address, Phone Number, and similar domain concepts.

---

## 5. Business Rules

Business invariants are implemented explicitly using the Business Rule pattern.

Every rule:

- Implements `IBusinessRule`.
- Inherits from `BusinessRule`.
- Provides a descriptive validation message.
- Is enforced through `AggregateRoot.CheckRule()`.

Violation of a business rule results in a `BusinessRuleValidationException`.

Business rules encapsulate business knowledge and eliminate scattered validation logic
throughout the domain.

---

## 6. Auditing

Aggregates requiring auditing inherit from `AuditableAggregateRoot`.

The auditing model consists of:

- CreatedOn
- CreatedBy
- ModifiedOn
- ModifiedBy
- DeletedOn
- DeletedBy
- IsDeleted

Auditing is considered an infrastructure concern exposed through the Domain model and will be
populated by the Infrastructure layer.

No business-specific properties shall be added to `AuditableAggregateRoot`.

---

## 7. Tenant Ownership

NexoraEnterprise follows the Tenant-Centric Architecture defined in ADR-001.

Tenant ownership is **not** part of the Domain Foundation.

`TenantId` will not be included in the base entity hierarchy.

Instead, tenant ownership will be modeled explicitly by aggregates that belong to a tenant.

This prevents unnecessary coupling and allows system-level aggregates to exist independently
of tenant ownership.

---

## 8. Domain Design Principles

The Domain layer shall remain:

- Persistence ignorant.
- Framework independent.
- Infrastructure independent.
- Focused solely on business behavior.
- Free from application, database, and API concerns.

The Domain model should express business intent rather than technical implementation details.

---

# Consequences

Adopting this Domain Foundation provides:

- A consistent entity hierarchy across the solution.
- Clear separation of entities and aggregate roots.
- Centralized business rule enforcement.
- Native support for domain events.
- Value-based equality for value objects.
- Built-in auditing support.
- A clean foundation for future business modules.
- Reduced architectural complexity.
- Improved maintainability and extensibility.

This foundation will be reused across all future modules within NexoraEnterprise.

---

# Alternatives Considered

### Introduce a `BaseEntity` class

Rejected.

An additional base class provided no behavior or responsibilities beyond the existing
`Entity` abstraction and unnecessarily complicated the inheritance hierarchy.

---

### Embed tenant ownership into the base hierarchy

Rejected.

Tenant ownership is a business concern rather than a foundational domain concern.

Keeping tenant ownership separate results in a cleaner and more flexible domain model.
